Natural Join Operator

The NATURAL JOIN operator combines data from more than one source. In DBSnap, you provide the NATURAL JOIN operator two datasets that have at least one matching column. For example, if you tried joining the data sets “Professors” and “Students” it wouldn’t work because they have no related columns. However, if you try to join the data sets “Course_Students” and “Students,” this would work because they both share a SID column.

NATURAL JOIN extends the number of columns we have available to us, which can help us identify the patterns in data. This will be useful when we incorporate aggregation.
